About Me

My Computer Science Journey began back in my junior year of high school when I planned on being an aspiring Engineer. But after taking an Intro to Programming class that used Visual Basic and Scratch, I decided to see what else the field had to offer by taking AP Mobile Computer Science Principles in my senior year along with a Web Design class. These classes really sparked my interest in Computer Science and coding in general that it made me decide to be a Computer Science major.

The journey continued when I took CSCI 1133 at the University of Minnesota, this class was taught in Python and it was a course meant to introduce students to basic programming concepts. I remember having a lot of fun in that class as every new topic was like equipping a new tool to my growing toolbox of computing knowledge.

Today, I am a junior majoring in Computer Science at the University of Minnesota Twin Cities, class of 2025. In addition to having learned Python, I have also learned languages such as Java and C to name a few. I also have some relevant experience by being a Teaching Assistant for a computer science course taught in Java, helping other students get started with their computer science journey similar to how I started out back then.

In my free time, I like to watch and play Tennis from time to time as one of my hobbies. I also enjoy biking around the beautiful University of Minnesota campus and the city of Minneapolis.
